How Indian users should prepare photos

To get the best redesign, start with a clean, well-lit room photo. Open curtains during daytime when possible, switch on warm or neutral lights, and take the picture from a corner that captures the whole room. In Indian homes, wires, laundry stands, buckets, folded mattresses, and temporary plastic chairs are common realities, so do not worry if the room is not magazine-ready. What matters is that the major elements are visible. The clearer the room photo, the easier it is to understand the scale and visual balance of the redesign. If your home has patterned tiles, darker wood, or multicolored storage units, AI outputs can also help you test whether it is better to contrast those surfaces or soften them with light fabrics, muted walls, and simpler accessories.

Choosing the right style for your city and lifestyle

Different styles solve different problems. Modern design works well for urban apartments because it creates a polished look with clean lines and structured furniture. Scandinavian design is excellent for darker rooms or homes that need a brighter, calmer feel with light wood and neutral colors. Minimalist design suits people who want visual quiet, less clutter, and easy maintenance in busy households. Industrial style works when you want stronger contrast, darker accents, and a slightly loft-inspired feel. Bohemian design fits creative spaces where textiles, layered decor, and personality matter more than strict symmetry. In hot cities like Ahmedabad or Chennai, many users prefer airy palettes and lighter fabrics. In cities like Bengaluru or Pune, where renters and young professionals move often, styles that work with portable furniture and minimal civil changes tend to be more practical.
